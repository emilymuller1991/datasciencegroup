Objectives

Wed Dec 27, 2017 by Data Science Group, AIMS, South Africa

The key object is to empower female students at AIMS South Africa to achieve their full potential. Furthermore, AIMSWIS intends to train a generation of women scientists as mentors to increase the number of role models for girls in African communities. Finally, AIMSWIS wishes to create a space for women students in mathematical sciences.

These objectives can be summarized in five key points:

  • Encourage women and girls to pursue careers in the STEM.
  • Supply AIMS women students and researchers with a network of successful women professionals in STEM including AIMS alumni.
  • Provide a platform for AIMS (both male and female) students to actively engage in the community by mentoring at local schools.
  • Provide regularly information to students outlining career and funding opportunities.
  • Create a safe space for women students where they obtain support from senior students and mentors who serve as role models and help them develop their life skills and build self-confidence. Within this space, ideas, challenges and opportunities can be shared, solutions can be sought and progress can be made.

Emphasis is not only put on being a mentee, but also becoming a mentor. This is encouraged by the support provided by AIMSWIS for AIMS students all the way until they become an alumna.

“The AIMS Women in Stem Initiative (AIMSWIS) is crucial and beneficial to Africa’s innovative capacity and global competitiveness since the social and economic inclusion of women plays a huge role in economic growth.” Ms. Palesa Motsoeneng, (Assistant Director, DST Multilateral Cooperation and Africa)
